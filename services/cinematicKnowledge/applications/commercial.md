# B2B & Commercial Content Application Guide

## Content Definition
B2B and commercial content includes corporate videos, brand films, testimonials, product demonstrations, training content, executive communications, and promotional business content.

---

## KEY VISUAL PRINCIPLES

### Primary Goals
1. **Trust building** - credibility through transparency
2. **Message clarity** - communication over style
3. **Professional positioning** - competence and authority
4. **Human connection** - business is still about people

---

## THE TRUST IMPERATIVE

### Visual Trust Signals
- **Eye level** = equality, honesty
- **High key lighting** = nothing to hide
- **Clear composition** = organized thinking
- **Professional execution** = competent company

### Visual Distrust Signals (Avoid)
- Dutch angles = instability
- Deep shadows = hiding something
- Chaotic composition = disorganized
- Amateur execution = incompetent company

---

## SHOT TYPE APPLICATIONS

### ECU (Extreme Close-Up)
- **Product detail** - feature showcase
- **Quality emphasis** - craftsmanship, precision
- **Technology** - innovation close-up
- **Brand mark** - logo, signature
- **Data/screen** - key information
- **Use sparingly** - only for critical detail

### CU (Close-Up)
- **Emotional testimony** - personal impact stories
- **Key message** - most important points
- **Human connection** - beyond corporate, the person
- **Reaction** - stakeholder response
- **Executive sincerity** - authentic leadership

### MCU (Medium Close-Up)
- **Key statement** - important message emphasis
- **Expert positioning** - authority and knowledge
- **Emotional testimonial** - personal story
- **Interview standard** - talking heads
- **Most common corporate shot**

### MS (Medium Shot)
- **Interview standard** - talking heads default
- **Presentation** - speaker engagement
- **Testimonial** - trust and credibility
- **Demonstration** - showing process
- **Two-person conversation**

### MWS (Medium Wide Shot)
- **Presenter shots** - speaker with presence
- **Professional stance** - authority without intimidation
- **Product in context** - item with user
- **Meeting scenes** - business interaction

### WS (Wide Shot)
- **Team shots** - groups working together
- **Facility context** - workspace environment
- **Process overview** - workflow visible
- **Location establishing** - where the business is
- **Scale demonstration** - showing capability

### EWS (Extreme Wide Shot)
- **Facility/campus** - company scale
- **Global presence** - locations, reach
- **Market context** - company in landscape
- **Drone establishing** - impressive scale
- **Opening shots** - brand world

---

## CAMERA ANGLE APPLICATIONS

### Eye Level (PRIMARY DEFAULT)
- **Trust building** - honest, no manipulation
- **Peer communication** - business equals
- **Testimonials** - authentic human connection
- **Default for interviews** - professional standard
- **90%+ of B2B content should be eye level**

### Low Angle (USE CAREFULLY)
- **Executive authority** - leadership positioning
- **Product superiority** - premium positioning
- **Company dominance** - market leadership
- **Use sparingly** - can feel manipulative

### High Angle (USE CAREFULLY)
- **Overview/context** - company in landscape
- **Organizational view** - teams, processes
- **Facility overview** - layout and scale
- **⚠️ CAUTION**: Can make executives appear weak

### Dutch Angle
- **⚠️ USE VERY RARELY** - appears unprofessional
- **Only for**: disruption messaging, innovation emphasis
- **Risk**: appearing unstable/untrustworthy
- **Most B2B should NEVER use dutch angles**

### Overhead
- **Organizational overview** - teams, processes
- **Facility tours** - layout and scale
- **Data visualization** - conceptual representation
- **Manufacturing** - process overview

---

## LIGHTING FOR B2B

### High Key (PRIMARY DEFAULT)
- **Trust and transparency** - nothing to hide
- **Positive messaging** - optimistic outlook
- **Professional standard** - clean and clear
- **Product showcase** - feature visibility
- **This should be your default**

### Soft Light
- **Interview lighting** - comfortable, flattering
- **Testimonials** - trustworthy appearance
- **Executive portraits** - professional but approachable
- **Product showcase** - detail without harshness

### Low Key (USE CAREFULLY)
- **Premium positioning** - luxury, exclusive
- **Serious topics** - gravitas and weight
- **Technology showcase** - dramatic reveals
- **⚠️ CAUTION**: Can feel secretive

### Hard Light (SITUATIONAL)
- **Documentary style** - authentic, real
- **Manufacturing** - industrial environments
- **⚠️ CAUTION**: Can be unflattering for people

### Color Temperature
- **Neutral (4000-5000K)** - professional standard, clarity
- **Slightly warm** - approachable, human
- **Cool** - technology emphasis, innovation

## BEAT STRUCTURE FOR B2B

### Corporate Video Structure
1. **Hook** (0-5s): Problem or attention-grabber
2. **Problem** (5-20s): Pain point establishment
3. **Solution** (20-60s): Your offering
4. **Proof** (60-90s): Evidence, testimonials, data
5. **Call to Action** (90-120s): What to do next

### Testimonial Structure
1. **Introduction** (0-10s): Who is this person?
2. **Problem** (10-30s): What challenge did they face?
3. **Solution** (30-60s): How did you help?
4. **Result** (60-90s): What was the outcome?
5. **Recommendation** (90-120s): Would they recommend?

### Product Demo Structure
1. **Problem context** (0-15s): Why this matters
2. **Product introduction** (15-30s): What it is
3. **Feature demonstration** (30-90s): How it works
4. **Benefit emphasis** (90-120s): Why it matters
5. **Call to action** (120s+): Next steps

---

## PRESENTER/SPEAKER BEST PRACTICES

### Framing
- MCU or MS for most delivery
- Tighten to CU for key points
- Widen for movement/demonstration
- Consistent eye line (looking at camera or interviewer)

### Backgrounds
- Clean, uncluttered
- Brand-appropriate
- Not distracting
- Professional environment

### Lighting
- Even, flattering
- Minimal shadows on face
- Background separation
- No harsh contrasts

---

## COMEDY/PERSONALITY IN B2B

### When to Use
- Brand personality content
- Social media clips
- Training video engagement
- Presenter humor

### Key Timing Rules
- **Pause after humor** - let it land
- **Keep it professional** - don't undermine credibility
- **Authentic over forced** - natural personality
- **Know your audience** - appropriate humor only

### When NOT to Use
- Serious topics (layoffs, crises, etc.)
- Highly technical content
- Formal executive communications
- When it would undermine trust

---

## PRODUCTION CONSIDERATIONS

### Priority Coverage
1. **Key message delivery** - main talking points
2. **Presenter/spokesperson** - face of the message
3. **Product/service** - what you're selling
4. **Proof points** - data, testimonials, evidence
5. **Call to action** - clear next step

### Interview Setup
- MCU standard framing
- Eye level camera
- 45° off-axis interviewer position
- Clean background
- Professional lighting

### B-Roll Needs
- Office/facility footage
- People working
- Product in use
- Technology/screens
- Team interactions

### Consistency Priorities
- **Brand colors** - consistent throughout
- **Lighting style** - same approach
- **Graphics/titles** - consistent design
- **Tone** - matches brand voice

### Common Challenges
- Executive availability and comfort
- Balancing professional with human
- Making complex topics clear
- Avoiding corporate clichés
- Maintaining engagement

## EXAMPLE SHOT BREAKDOWNS

### Executive Message
1. MS - Executive at desk/location (3s)
2. MCU - Key message delivery (varies)
3. B-roll - Business activity (3s)
4. Return to MCU - Continue message
5. CU - Most important point (2s)
6. MS - Call to action, close (3s)

### Customer Testimonial
1. MS - Customer introduction (5s)
2. MCU - Problem description (15s)
3. B-roll - Their business (3s)
4. MCU - Solution/working with you (20s)
5. CU - Results/emotional moment (10s)
6. MS - Recommendation (10s)

### Product Demo
1. MS - Presenter introduction (5s)
2. WS - Product in context (3s)
3. MS - Feature demonstration (varies)
4. ECU - Detail close-up (2s)
5. MS - Benefit explanation (10s)
6. MCU - Call to action (5s)

### Company Overview
1. Drone EWS - Facility establishing (5s)
2. WS - Team activity (3s)
3. MS - Leader/spokesperson (10s)
4. WS - Production/capability (5s)
5. MCU - Customer testimonial snippet (10s)
6. MS - Executive close (5s)
7. Brand end card
