# Music Video Application Guide

## Content Definition
Music video content spans performance capture, narrative storytelling, visual albums, lyric videos, and promotional artist content. The unique aspect is synchronization with musical structure.

---

## KEY VISUAL PRINCIPLES

### Primary Goals
1. **Artist as icon** - aspirational positioning
2. **Emotional amplification** - visual enhances audio
3. **Beat synchronization** - cuts and movement match music
4. **Genre expression** - visual language matches musical style

---

## SHOT TYPE APPLICATIONS

### ECU (Extreme Close-Up)
- **Lips singing** - intimate lyric delivery
- **Eyes** - soulful connection, emotion
- **Hands on instrument** - musical skill
- **Abstract moments** - artistic expression
- **Texture/detail** - artistic emphasis

### CU (Close-Up)
- **Emotional peak** - most intense lyric delivery
- **Intimacy** - connection with audience
- **Vulnerability** - raw, exposed moment
- **Key lyric emphasis** - important words

### MCU (Medium Close-Up)
- **Emotional delivery** - intimate performance
- **Reaction shots** - feeling the music
- **Connection** - artist to audience
- **Standard performance coverage**

### MS (Medium Shot)
- **Standard performance** - artist engagement
- **Emotional delivery** - lyrics and expression
- **Band member isolation** - individual focus
- **Instrument visibility**

### MWS (Medium Wide Shot)
- **Performance stance** - artist presence
- **Walking shots** - movement and style
- **Instrument visibility** - musician with gear
- **Choreography elements**

### WS (Wide Shot)
- **Performance context** - artist on stage
- **Dance/movement** - full choreography
- **Band shot** - multiple performers
- **Location context** - where action happens

### EWS (Extreme Wide Shot)
- **Concert scale** - crowd, venue, atmosphere
- **Location establishing** - where the story happens
- **Isolation/loneliness** - artist alone in vast space
- **Journey narrative** - travel, movement
- **Epic scope**

## BEAT STRUCTURE FOR MUSIC VIDEO

### Song Structure Mapping
1. **Intro** (Setup): Establish scene, mood, character
2. **Verse 1** (Development): Story begins, build
3. **Pre-Chorus** (Escalation): Tension building
4. **Chorus** (Climax): Maximum visual intensity
5. **Verse 2** (Development): Story continues
6. **Chorus 2** (Climax): Bigger than first
7. **Bridge** (Turning Point): Visual shift, new element
8. **Final Chorus** (Peak Climax): Maximum everything
9. **Outro** (Resolution): Wind down, final image

### Visual Intensity Mapping
- **Intro**: Wide, establishing, slow
- **Verse**: Medium shots, story development
- **Pre-Chorus**: Tightening, building energy
- **Chorus**: Tightest shots, fastest cuts, maximum energy
- **Bridge**: Visual surprise, different approach
- **Outro**: Pull back, slow down, resolve

## EDITING & TIMING

### Cut on Beat
- Shot changes sync with musical beats
- Accents emphasized with cuts
- Rhythm of editing matches rhythm of song

### Hold Through Beat
- Longer takes for emotional moments
- Build tension by NOT cutting
- Let performance breathe

### Duration by Song Section

| Section | Cut Rhythm | Shot Size | Energy |
|---------|------------|-----------|--------|
| Intro | 4-8 sec | Wide | Low |
| Verse | 2-4 sec | Medium | Building |
| Pre-Chorus | 1-2 sec | Tightening | High |
| Chorus | 0.5-2 sec | Close | Maximum |
| Bridge | Variable | Variable | Shift |
| Outro | 4-8 sec | Widening | Decreasing |

## GENRE-SPECIFIC APPROACHES

### Pop
- High key lighting, clean beauty
- Performance energy, choreography
- Aspirational, bright aesthetic
- Dynamic camera movement

### Hip-Hop/R&B
- Low key dramatic lighting
- Low angles for power
- Confident, dominant positioning
- Lifestyle/luxury elements

### Rock/Alternative
- Hard lighting, high contrast
- Raw, authentic energy
- Documentary elements
- Performance intensity

### Electronic/Dance
- Colored lighting, neon
- Dutch angles, experimental
- Strobing, dynamic
- Abstract visuals

### Ballad/Emotional
- Soft lighting, intimate
- Close-ups for emotion
- Slower cuts, breathing room
- Natural, authentic feeling

## COMEDY/PLAYFUL TIMING

### When to Use
- Comedy music videos (clear genre)
- Playful moments in performance
- Behind-the-scenes content
- Social media clips

### Key Timing Rules
- Reaction shots held 2-3 seconds
- Rule of three still applies
- Beat sync for comedic emphasis
- Timing can be musical or traditional

## PRODUCTION CONSIDERATIONS

### Priority Coverage
1. **Performance master** - full song, safety coverage
2. **Close-up performance** - face, emotion, lyrics
3. **Band/musician coverage** - all performers
4. **Story/narrative shots** - if applicable
5. **B-roll/detail** - texture, environment

### Consistency Priorities
- Artist appearance throughout
- Performance energy consistency
- Location/time continuity
- Wardrobe changes (if intentional)

### Common Challenges
- Lip sync accuracy
- Performance energy consistency
- Multiple outfit/location management
- Lighting changes for mood shifts

## EXAMPLE SHOT BREAKDOWNS

### Performance-Based Music Video
1. EWS - Location establishing (4s) - intro
2. WS - Artist enters frame (3s)
3. MS - Performance begins (4s) - verse
4. MCU - Emotional delivery (2s)
5. CU - Key lyric emphasis (1s) - pre-chorus
6. Rapid cuts between angles (chorus)
7. Return to MS (2s) - verse 2
8. Continue pattern, intensifying...

### Narrative Music Video
1. EWS - World establishing (4s)
2. MS - Character introduction (3s)
3. Story development through verses
4. CU artist - emotional chorus
5. Intercut story and performance
6. Story climax aligned with song climax
7. Resolution in outro

### Choreography-Heavy
1. WS - Full formation (3s)
2. Overhead - Pattern view (2s)
3. MS - Lead dancer (2s)
4. MCU - Face/expression (1s)
5. WS - Key move (2s)
6. Rapid coverage through chorus
7. Final formation reveal
