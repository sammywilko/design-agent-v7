# Sport-Fashion Content Application Guide

## Content Definition
Sport-fashion bridges athletic performance with lifestyle/fashion aesthetics. Includes athlete campaigns, athleisure brands, sporting goods, fitness content, and sport-lifestyle crossover.

---

## KEY VISUAL PRINCIPLES

### The Athlete Hero Shot
**Primary Goal**: Make athletes look powerful, aspirational, and human

#### Camera Approach
- **Low angles** for heroism and power (pre-competition, victory)
- **Eye level** for relatability and human story
- **High angles** for tactical overview or vulnerability moments

#### Shot Size Priority
1. **WS** - Athletic action, full body movement, outfit showcase
2. **MS** - Training dialogue, product in use, testimonials
3. **CU** - Determination, emotion, intensity, game face
4. **MWS** - Athleisure showcase, walking shots, stance

#### Lighting Mood
- **High key** for aspirational lifestyle, positive energy
- **Low key** for gritty determination, intense training
- **Golden hour** for premium aesthetic, warmth
- **Hard/dramatic** for peak competition moments

## SHOT TYPE APPLICATIONS

### ECU (Extreme Close-Up)
- Sweat on skin - determination, effort
- Eyes of champion - focus, intensity
- Hands gripping equipment - skill, preparation
- Texture of gear/apparel - quality, technology

### CU (Close-Up)
- Game face - pre-competition intensity
- Victory/defeat emotion - human story
- Focus and determination - mental strength
- Brand ambassador face - personality

### MCU (Medium Close-Up)
- Interview emphasis - key statements
- Reaction shots - celebration, frustration
- Pre-game intensity - athlete ready
- Coach/athlete interaction

### MS (Medium Shot)
- Training dialogue - coaching moments
- Product showcase - gear in use
- Interview standard - testimonials
- Upper body technique - form, skill

### MWS (Medium Wide Shot)
- Athletic stance - ready position
- Athleisure showcase - outfit head to mid-thigh
- Walking shots - movement and style
- Pre-action moments - preparing

### WS (Wide Shot)
- Full athletic movement - technique visible
- Training context - athlete in environment
- Head-to-toe outfit - complete look
- Team/group dynamics

### EWS (Extreme Wide Shot)
- Stadium/arena scale - epic atmosphere
- Training landscape - athlete in world
- Journey shots - running across terrain
- Scale of achievement - tiny figure, massive context

## BEAT STRUCTURE FOR SPORT-FASHION

### Typical Narrative Arc
1. **Setup**: Athlete's challenge, starting point, "before"
2. **Development**: Training progression, preparation, building
3. **Turning Point**: Competition moment, breakthrough, pivot
4. **Climax**: Victory/achievement, peak performance
5. **Resolution**: New champion state, transformation complete

### Short-Form Structure (15-60 sec)
1. **Hook** (0-3s): Intense action or striking athlete shot
2. **Setup** (3-10s): Challenge established
3. **Development** (10-30s): Training/preparation montage
4. **Payoff** (30-50s): Victory/achievement moment
5. **Button** (50-60s): Brand moment, aspirational close

---

## COMEDY TIMING IN SPORT CONTENT

### When to Use
- Behind-the-scenes personality content
- Athlete reactions to unexpected moments
- Social media personality clips
- Brand personality building

### Key Timing Rules
- Hold athlete reactions 2-3 seconds
- Use rule of three: "Train hard. Eat clean. Fall asleep in your gear."
- Celebration/surprise moments need breathing room
- Blooper moments need pause before continuing

## PRODUCTION CONSIDERATIONS

### Priority Shot Coverage
1. **Hero action** - key athletic moment
2. **Product in use** - gear/apparel showcase
3. **Athlete personality** - human connection
4. **Environment establishing** - world building
5. **Detail/texture** - quality emphasis

### Consistency Priorities
- Athletic build consistency
- Outfit continuity (same gear across shots)
- Environment consistency
- Action sequence continuity

### Common Challenges
- Athlete availability - limited time
- Weather for outdoor shoots
- Action timing - unpredictable moments
- Brand placement accuracy

## EXAMPLE SHOT BREAKDOWNS

### Athlete Training Sequence
1. EWS - Training facility establishing (3s)
2. WS - Athlete enters, begins warmup (4s)
3. MS - Focused preparation, adjusting gear (3s)
4. MCU - Determination on face (2s)
5. WS - Full athletic movement, technique (4s)
6. CU - Intensity, effort, sweat (2s)
7. MS - Completion, satisfaction (3s)

### Competition Victory Moment
1. MS - Pre-competition tension (3s)
2. WS - Event action (5s)
3. MCU - Intense focus (2s)
4. CU - Victory realization (2s) - HOLD
5. WS - Celebration, arms up (4s)
6. Low angle CU - Champion face (3s)

### Product Showcase
1. WS - Athlete in environment with product (4s)
2. MS - Product in use during action (3s)
3. ECU - Product detail, quality (2s)
4. MCU - Athlete appreciation of gear (2s)
5. WS - Full action demonstrating product (4s)
