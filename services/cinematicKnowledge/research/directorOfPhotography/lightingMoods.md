# Lighting Mood Psychology

## The Science
Lighting triggers primal survival instincts. We're hardwired to feel safe in even lighting (can see threats) and uneasy in shadows (hidden dangers).

## Lighting Qualities

### HIGH KEY (Bright, Even)
- **Look**: Minimal shadows, bright overall, soft contrast
- **Psychology**: Safety, optimism, clarity, openness, nothing to hide
- **Neuroscience**: Mimics daylight - activates "safe to explore" responses
- **Use For**:
  - Comedy
  - Corporate positivity
  - Product beauty
  - Utopian/ideal settings
  - Children's content
- **Technical**: Fill light near key intensity, soft sources, white/light backgrounds

### LOW KEY (Dark, Dramatic)
- **Look**: Strong shadows, high contrast, selective illumination
- **Psychology**: Mystery, danger, drama, secrets, sophistication
- **Neuroscience**: Darkness triggers vigilance - primitive "what's hiding there?" response
- **Use For**:
  - Thriller/noir
  - Luxury products
  - Dramatic reveals
  - Villain scenes
  - Serious topics
- **Technical**: Minimal fill, hard sources, dark backgrounds, motivated practicals

### SOFT LIGHT
- **Look**: Gradual shadow transitions, diffused, flattering
- **Psychology**: Gentleness, beauty, romance, approachability, timelessness
- **Neuroscience**: Cloudy day lighting - comfortable, no harsh judgment
- **Use For**:
  - Beauty/fashion
  - Romance
  - Interviews
  - Product glamour
  - Sensitive topics
- **Technical**: Large sources, diffusion, bounce, overcast simulation

### HARD LIGHT
- **Look**: Sharp shadows, defined edges, dramatic contrast
- **Psychology**: Intensity, truth, harshness, no escape, interrogation
- **Neuroscience**: Direct sun = judgment, exposure, nowhere to hide
- **Use For**:
  - Confrontation
  - Documentary truth
  - Gritty reality
  - High fashion edge
  - Thriller tension
- **Technical**: Small sources, minimal diffusion, Fresnel lenses

## Light Direction Psychology

### FRONT LIGHT
- **Effect**: Flat, minimal shadows, full visibility
- **Mood**: Neutral, informational, surveillance
- **Use**: News, mugshots, clinical

### SIDE LIGHT (Rembrandt)
- **Effect**: Half shadow, sculpted, dimensional
- **Mood**: Drama, depth, artistic, classical
- **Use**: Portraits, drama, revealing character

### BACK LIGHT
- **Effect**: Rim light, silhouette potential, separation
- **Mood**: Mystery, ethereal, separation from background
- **Use**: Hero reveals, romantic moments, dream sequences

### UNDER LIGHT
- **Effect**: Unnatural, horror, campfire
- **Mood**: Fear, supernatural, sinister
- **Use**: Horror, villain moments, ghost stories
- **Note**: Rarely flattering - use with extreme intention

### TOP LIGHT
- **Effect**: Pools of light, theatrical, eye shadows
- **Mood**: Interrogation, divine, stage
- **Use**: Confession scenes, religious imagery, theater

## Color Temperature Psychology

### WARM (2700K-3500K)
- **Color**: Orange/yellow
- **Psychology**: Comfort, intimacy, sunset, nostalgia, safety
- **Use**: Home scenes, romance, memory/flashback, cozy environments

### NEUTRAL (4000K-5000K)
- **Color**: White
- **Psychology**: Reality, present moment, clarity, professional
- **Use**: Documentary, corporate, "normal" reality

### COOL (5500K-7500K)
- **Color**: Blue
- **Psychology**: Technology, cold, clinical, future, isolation, sadness
- **Use**: Sci-fi, hospitals, loneliness, winter, digital environments

### MIXED (Warm + Cool)
- **Effect**: Contrast, visual interest, time of day
- **Psychology**: Transition, complexity, real-world authenticity
- **Use**: Day-for-night, city scenes, dramatic contrast

## Lighting by Scene Type

| Scene Type | Key Quality | Direction | Temperature |
|-----------|-------------|-----------|-------------|
| Comedy | High key | Front/side | Warm-neutral |
| Drama | Low-mid key | Side | Variable |
| Thriller | Low key | Side/back | Cool |
| Romance | Soft | Back/side | Warm |
| Horror | Contrast | Under/side | Cool with warm accents |
| Corporate | High key | Front/side | Neutral |
| Product Beauty | Soft | Side/back | Neutral-warm |
| Documentary | Natural | Available | As found |

## Time of Day Lighting

### GOLDEN HOUR
- Warm, soft, directional
- Romantic, magical, nostalgic
- Limited window - plan carefully

### BLUE HOUR
- Cool, soft, ethereal
- Mysterious, transitional, melancholy
- Even shorter window than golden hour

### MIDDAY
- Harsh, overhead, unflattering
- Typically avoided for beauty
- Good for gritty realism

### NIGHT
- Artificial sources, high contrast
- Noir, danger, isolation
- Requires significant lighting setup

## Practical Lighting Notes
Practicals (visible light sources) add:
- **Realism**: Motivated light sources
- **Depth**: Layers of illumination
- **Production value**: Visual interest
- **Mood anchors**: Warm lamps = cozy, fluorescents = clinical
